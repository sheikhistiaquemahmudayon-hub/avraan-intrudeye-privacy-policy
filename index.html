Privacy Policy for Avraan IntrudEye

Last Updated: June 16, 2026

Avraan IntrudEye is designed to help users protect their devices from unauthorized access. We believe security should never come at the cost of privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what information is processed, why it is needed, and how it is protected.

Imagine leaving your phone on a desk, in a classroom, or while traveling. If someone attempts to unlock your device without permission, IntrudEye can capture evidence and notify you. To provide these anti-theft features, the app requires access to certain device permissions and security-related information.

Information We Collect

To provide anti-theft protection and security monitoring, IntrudEye may collect and process the following information:

  • Unique installation identifier (Installation ID)
  • Application version and build information
  • Device platform details (Manufacturer, Model, and OS version)
  • First active and last active timestamps
  • Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) token
  • Security alert logs and metadata
  • Failed unlock attempt records
  • Intruder photos captured during failed unlock attempts
  • Location coordinates associated with security alerts
  • Premium subscription status (if applicable)

Camera Permission & Photo Handling

IntrudEye uses the Camera permission to capture a photo when an unauthorized unlock attempt is detected. Captured photos help identify who attempted to access the device and are used exclusively for anti-theft and device security purposes.

When a security event occurs:

  • A photo is captured automatically using the front-facing camera.
  • The photo is stored within the application's secure local directory on your device.
  • The photo is securely uploaded to Firebase Storage under your account to enable cross-device synchronization and let you view intruder logs from the web admin panel.
  • The photo may also be transmitted directly to the recipient email address configured in the app settings.

Avraan does not operate any third-party advertising photo-storage servers, and we do not sell, rent, or share your captured photos with any third parties or advertising networks.

Location Permission

IntrudEye uses precise location, approximate location, and background location access to determine where a security event occurred. Background location is required because unauthorized unlock attempts may occur while the device is locked and the application is not actively open.

Location information is used solely for:

  • Security alerts and map links
  • Device recovery assistance
  • Anti-theft protection

SMS & Phone Permissions (Offline Protection)

IntrudEye requests the Send SMS and Read Phone State permissions to ensure your device remains protected even when disconnected from the internet:

  • SMS Permission (SEND_SMS): Used to send outbound emergency security alerts (containing device location coordinates and carrier info) to your pre-configured emergency phone number when the device is locked and offline.
  • Phone/SIM Permission (READ_PHONE_STATE): Used to check active cellular connections and identify SIM card details so that offline SMS alerts can be reliably routed using your preferred primary or fallback SIM card (useful for dual-SIM devices).

Device Administrator Permission

IntrudEye uses Android Device Administrator capabilities to support anti-theft protection and device security functionality. This permission is used to monitor lockscreen lock attempts and detect failed password, PIN, or pattern entries.

IntrudEye does not use Device Administrator access to:

  • Change user passwords
  • Erase device data
  • Access personal files, messages, or contacts

Users may revoke Device Administrator access at any time through Android Settings.

Firebase Services

IntrudEye uses selected Google Firebase services to support application functionality and user security:

  • Firebase Authentication: To securely manage your user account.
  • Firebase Storage: To store and backup captured intruder snapshots.
  • Firebase Firestore: To sync security alert logs and device configuration.
  • Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM): To receive notifications and security updates.
  • Firebase Remote Config: To update application parameters dynamically.
  • Firebase Crashlytics: To collect anonymous performance reports and fix crashes.

Information Stored in Firebase

Depending on app usage, Firebase services may store:

  • Installation identifiers
  • Notification tokens
  • Security event logs
  • Alert metadata
  • Failed unlock records (including intruder photos)
  • Location-related alert coordinates
  • Premium subscription information

This information is used solely to provide application functionality, anti-theft protection, and service reliability.

Information We Do Not Collect

IntrudEye does not collect or access:

  • Contacts list
  • Personal SMS messages (we only send security alerts to your configured emergency contact; we never read, monitor, or collect incoming or stored SMS messages)
  • Call logs or phone calls (we only read SIM status for offline SMS alerts; we never record calls or access call records)
  • Clipboard content
  • Browsing history
  • Advertising IDs
  • Payment card details
  • Social media credentials

IntrudEye does not sell personal information to advertisers or data brokers.

Data Security

We take modern, industry-standard security measures to protect the information processed by the application. However, no method of electronic storage or transmission can guarantee absolute security.

Children's Privacy

IntrudEye is not intended for children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

Your Choices

  • Disable camera, location, SMS, or phone state permissions through Android Settings.
  • Configure, update, or disable Email, Telegram, or SMS alert recipients in the app settings.
  • Disable IntrudEye monitoring features.
  • Revoke Device Administrator access.
  • Disable Accessibility Service access.
  • Uninstall the application at any time.
