Privacy Policy for Avraan IntrudEye

Last Updated: June 3, 2026

Avraan IntrudEye is designed to help users protect their devices from unauthorized access. We believe security should never come at the cost of privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what information is processed, why it is needed, and how it is protected.

Imagine leaving your phone on a desk, in a classroom, or while traveling. If someone attempts to unlock your device without permission, IntrudEye can capture evidence and notify you. To provide these anti-theft features, the app requires access to certain device permissions and security-related information.

Information We Collect

To provide anti-theft protection and security monitoring, IntrudEye may collect and process the following information:

  • Unique installation identifier (Installation ID)
  • Application version and build information
  • Device platform information
  • First active and last active timestamps
  • Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) token
  • Security alert logs and metadata
  • Failed unlock attempt records
  • Location coordinates associated with security alerts
  • Premium subscription status (if applicable)

Camera Permission

IntrudEye uses the Camera permission to capture a photo when an unauthorised unlock attempt is detected.

Captured photos help identify who attempted to access the device and are used exclusively for anti-theft and device security purposes.

Photo Handling

When a security event occurs:

  • A photo may be captured on the device.
  • The photo may be stored within the application's local storage area on the device.
  • The photo may be transmitted directly to the email address configured by the user.

Captured photos are not uploaded to Firebase Storage.

Avraan does not operate dedicated photo-storage servers and does not sell or share captured photos for advertising purposes.

Location Permission

IntrudEye may use precise location, approximate location, and background location access to determine where a security event occurred.

Background location is required because unauthorized unlock attempts may occur while the device is locked and the application is not actively open.

Location information is used solely for:

  • Security alerts
  • Device recovery assistance
  • Anti-theft protection

Device Administrator Permission

IntrudEye may use Android Device Administrator capabilities to support anti-theft protection and device security functionality.

This permission may be used to:

  • Detect failed unlock attempts
  • Monitor unauthorized access attempts
  • Trigger anti-theft protection features
  • Support security alerts and intruder detection

IntrudEye does not use Device Administrator access to:

  • Change user passwords
  • Erase device data
  • Access personal files
  • Access contacts or messages

Users may revoke Device Administrator access at any time through Android Settings.

Accessibility Service

IntrudEye may use Android Accessibility Service exclusively for anti-theft and device security functionality.

Certain Android devices apply aggressive battery optimisation and background execution restrictions that may affect the reliable delivery of security-related events. This behavior is commonly observed on some customized Android operating systems.

To improve reliability and maintain consistent protection across supported devices, IntrudEye may use Accessibility Service as a supplementary security mechanism.

Accessibility Service may be used solely for:

  • Detecting unauthorized unlock attempts
  • Supporting anti-theft protection features
  • Improving security-event reliability
  • Delivering timely security alerts

IntrudEye does not use Accessibility Service to:

  • Read personal messages
  • Read emails
  • Capture passwords
  • Record keystrokes
  • Monitor browsing activity
  • Access personal files
  • Track activity unrelated to device security

Accessibility data is not sold, shared for advertising purposes, or used for user profiling.

The Accessibility Service is not used to monitor user activity outside the anti-theft and device security functionality provided by IntrudEye.

Firebase Services

IntrudEye uses selected Google Firebase services to support application functionality.

  • Firebase Firestore
  • Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM)
  • Firebase Remote Config

These services help support:

  • Security alert synchronization
  • Device registration
  • Notification delivery
  • Application configuration

Information Stored in Firebase

Depending on app usage, Firebase services may store:

  • Installation identifiers
  • Notification tokens
  • Security event logs
  • Alert metadata
  • Failed unlock records
  • Location-related alert information
  • Premium subscription information

This information is used solely to provide application functionality, anti-theft protection, and service reliability.

Information We Do Not Collect

IntrudEye does not collect:

  • Contacts
  • SMS messages
  • Call logs
  • Clipboard content
  • Browsing history
  • Advertising IDs
  • Payment card information
  • Social media account credentials

IntrudEye does not sell personal information to advertisers or data brokers.

Data Security

We take reasonable measures to protect information processed by the application. However, no method of electronic storage or transmission can guarantee absolute security.

Children's Privacy

IntrudEye is not intended for children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

Your Choices

  • Disable permissions through Android Settings
  • Disable IntrudEye protection features
  • Revoke Device Administrator access
  • Disable Accessibility Service access
  • Uninstall the application at any time
